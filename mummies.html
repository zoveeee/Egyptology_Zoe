The Mummification Process

Ancient Egyptians believed in an afterlife when someone died. Mummification helped someone reach the afterlife as they believed that an afterlife could only exist if there was a form the ka (soul) could repossess after death. Egyptians believed that the only way to do this was if the body was recognisable. This is why they spent so long on the process of mummification and why Pharaohs began the building of their tombs during their lifetimes.

This is the step-by-step process of how mummification took place:

Insert a hook through a hole near the nose and pull out part of the brain

9 mins

Make a cut on the left side of the body near the tummy.

9 mins

Remove all internal organs and let the internal organs dry.

9 mins

Place the lungs, intestines, stomach and liver inside jars, then place the heart back inside.

9 mins

Afterwards, Rinse inside of body with wine and spices.

9 mins

After 40 days stuff the body with linen or sand to give it a more human shape.

9 mins

After the 70 days wrap the body from head to toe in bandages.

9 mins

Place in a sarcophagus (a type of box like a coffin).

9 mins

If the person had been a Pharaoh, he would be placed inside a special burial chamber with lots of treasure.

9 mins
